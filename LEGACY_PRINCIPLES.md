# LEGACY Principles

Product Vision: Virgo Davis

Architecture: ChatGPT

Engineering: Cody

LEGACY Principles is the constitution for ChatCRM's intelligence layer. It defines how we decide what belongs in LEGACY, how recommendations must be supported, and how the product should feel as it grows.

## Mission

LEGACY transforms property data into actionable market intelligence.

Every screen, feature, and service should help users understand a property's opportunity faster and with greater confidence.

## 1. Every Conclusion Must Have Evidence

LEGACY never asks users to trust a black box.

Every recommendation, score, or narrative must be explainable and traceable to supporting data.

Good:

> "ABC Builders is the strongest buyer because they purchased four nearby parcels within the last 60 days."

Bad:

> "AI thinks this is the best buyer."

## 2. The Property Is The Center Of The Experience

LEGACY begins with an address.

Everything else - buyers, transactions, ownership, contacts, pricing, parcel data, and market activity - exists to explain that property.

## 3. The Map Is The Primary Interface

The map is not decoration.

It is the user's workspace.

Every new capability should improve spatial understanding, reveal relationships, or reduce research.

## 4. Every Click Reveals Intelligence

Interactions should uncover information, not simply navigate.

Clicking should answer questions such as:

- Why?
- Who?
- When?
- How often?
- What is nearby?
- What changed?

## 5. Never Make Users Research What LEGACY Already Knows

If data exists, LEGACY should connect it.

Instead of asking users to compare spreadsheets, search multiple websites, or perform manual calculations, LEGACY should synthesize the answer automatically.

## 6. Reduce Decisions

Success is not measured by how much data we display.

Success is measured by how quickly users know what to do next.

Every workspace should answer:

- Should we pursue this?
- What is it worth?
- Who should we contact first?
- Why?

## 7. Intelligence Over Information

Displaying information is not enough.

LEGACY should identify patterns, relationships, and opportunities that are difficult for users to discover on their own.

## 8. Build For Texas. Architect For The United States.

Provider integrations, parcel systems, and county-specific logic should remain modular.

The architecture should scale nationally without major redesign.

## 9. Confidence Matters

Every insight should communicate its certainty.

Users should understand what is:

- verified
- inferred
- estimated
- awaiting additional data

## 10. Earn Trust

Every release should make users more confident in LEGACY.

If an insight cannot be supported, it should not be presented as fact.

## Product Philosophy

LEGACY should feel like you are working alongside the best acquisitions manager on your team, not using another piece of software.

When someone opens a property, they should not feel like they are staring at a database. They should feel like an experienced teammate has already done the first hour of research and is walking them through the opportunity.

## Decision Filter

Before building any future feature, ask:

> What Market Intelligence does this consume or produce?

If the answer is unclear, question whether the feature belongs in ChatCRM.

The goal is not feature count.

The goal is one unified intelligence engine powering every workspace.

## Operating Standard

Keep thinking in systems.

Keep modules small.

Keep everything explainable.

No black boxes.

Every recommendation should have evidence behind it.

LEGACY should compound over years.
